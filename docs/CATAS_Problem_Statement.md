# CATAS: Compliance And Treasurer Agentic Solutions
## Research-Backed Problem Statement

---

## Executive Summary

Payment fraud continues to rise, with nearly 80% of companies reporting incidents in 2024, and treasury workflows are especially vulnerable due to transaction volume and the risk of approval bypasses. Simultaneously, treasury teams operate in environments where transparency, accountability, and compliance aren't optional—every transaction must be traceable and every decision must withstand public scrutiny. Yet many treasury teams are still relying on spreadsheets and manual processes to manage billions in cash and investments, which is dangerous.

The core problem: **Compliance and treasury teams operate in silos using fragmented, manual workflows that create blind spots, delay critical reporting, and expose organizations to fraud, regulatory penalties, and operational inefficiency.**

---

## The Problem: Three Interconnected Failures

### 1. **Manual Reconciliation Bottleneck**

**The Reality:**
- Manual reconciliation processes require companies to cut monthly reconciliation hours from 20 to under 5 through automation, but most are still doing this manually.
- Manual bank reconciliation remains a tedious, error-prone process that slows down financial operations and affects decision-making.
- Manual reconciliation increases the risk of errors, potentially leading to financial discrepancies and compliance issues.

**Business Impact:**
- Finance teams lose 15-25 hours/month on matching transactions manually
- Errors slip through unnoticed (data entry mistakes, duplicate entries, timing mismatches)
- Month-end close takes 3-5 days instead of real-time visibility
- No audit trail linking transactions to approval decisions

---

### 2. **Fragmented Compliance and Audit Trail Systems**

**The Reality:**
- Without structured approval visibility, accounts payable processes become vulnerable to errors and accountability gaps.
- Companies may keep data separate between departments or divisions, creating data silos where audit trail information is scattered or incomplete.
- With multi-jurisdictional compliance, every regulator has their own set of specifications, requirements, and nuances, meaning 2-3 times the burden and demand per jurisdiction.

**Business Impact:**
- Compliance teams cannot quickly answer: "Who approved this transaction and why?"
- Regulatory audits take weeks to prepare because audit trails are fragmented
- Non-compliance penalties: up to $5M+ fines and reputational damage
- Zero real-time visibility into approval workflows across departments

---

### 3. **Disconnect Between Treasury and Compliance Functions**

**The Reality:**
- Treasury focuses on cash positioning, liquidity forecasting, and working capital optimization
- Compliance focuses on regulatory reporting, transaction validation, and fraud detection
- Manual treasury operations and compliance are now introducing more risk than they prevent, yet teams resist automation because "automation removes human oversight" and "AI decisions can't be audited."
- Treasury automation solutions lack scalability, especially for multi-subsidiary businesses operating across jurisdictions, because data integration remains fractured.

**Business Impact:**
- Compliance blocks treasury innovations due to audit concerns
- Treasury cannot execute fast cash decisions (requires waiting for compliance sign-off)
- Duplicate data entry across systems (bank feeds ingested twice, different formats)
- Regulatory reporting delays because treasury and compliance data don't align

---

## The Research Landscape: Why Current Solutions Fall Short

### **Systemic Issues with Existing Approaches**

1. **Legacy Treasury Management Systems (TMS):**
   - Legacy platforms like SAP S/4HANA and ION Treasury require 12-24 months to implement and lack agentic AI, AI-powered forecasting engines, or strategic AI roadmaps comparable to leading platforms.
   - Monolithic systems cannot adapt to compliance rule changes without weeks of reconfiguration
   - Manual reconciliation still requires human effort despite "automated" features

2. **Compliance-Only Tools:**
   - Point solutions for audit trails, fraud detection, or reporting don't integrate with treasury data
   - No feedback loop between compliance decisions and treasury actions
   - Cannot handle real-time multi-jurisdictional rule application

3. **The AI/Automation Trust Gap:**
   - AI adoption in treasury is still cautious because of the high standards of governance, compliance and control that the function demands.
   - Current automation solutions lack explainability—regulators demand "why was this transaction approved?"
   - No audit trail showing which agent made which decision and based on what data

## CATAS Problem Definition: The Gap to Fill

**For treasury and compliance teams managing multi-entity, multi-jurisdiction cash and regulatory operations:**

CATAS is an **agentic automation platform** that orchestrates transaction validation, compliance rule enforcement, and regulatory reporting in real-time with complete audit trails and explainable AI decisions.

**Unlike existing solutions, CATAS:**
1. **Eliminates reconciliation lag** through multi-agent orchestration (ingest → validate → match → reconcile in <5 minutes)
2. **Provides explainable compliance** via audit trails that show which agent approved what and why (ACE-V governance)
3. **Bridges treasury and compliance** by creating a shared, real-time data foundation both teams trust
4. **Handles multi-jurisdictional complexity** through rule-engine agents that adapt to regulatory changes without recoding
5. **Detects fraud in real-time** via ML-powered anomaly detection combined with compliance rules
